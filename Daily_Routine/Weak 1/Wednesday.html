Shoulders

Overhead barbell press, also known as the military press, is a compound exercise that targets the shoulders, triceps, and upper chest. It involves pressing a barbell overhead while maintaining proper posture. This movement enhances shoulder strength, stability, and overall upper-body power.

Dumbbell Lateral Raises This isolation exercise targets the lateral deltoids by lifting dumbbells outward to shoulder height. It helps develop broader shoulders and improves muscular definition. Maintaining controlled movement is key to maximizing effectiveness and preventing injury.

Arnold Press Named after Arnold Schwarzenegger, the Arnold press engages all three heads of the deltoid. The exercise involves a twisting motion while pressing dumbbells overhead, increasing shoulder mobility and strength.

Front Plate Raises This exercise primarily targets the front deltoids by lifting a weight plate straight in front of the body. It enhances shoulder endurance, stability, and control while also engaging the upper chest and arms.

Rear Delt Flys (Dumbbells) Rear delt flys strengthen the posterior deltoids and upper back by lifting dumbbells outward while bending forward. This movement improves posture, shoulder balance, and rear delt development.
